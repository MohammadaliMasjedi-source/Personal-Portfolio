Mo
— Mo

I'm Mohammadali “Mo” Masjedi — from Shiraz, now in the Harz mountains of Germany.

By trade I'm a software engineer — I build data-analytics and IoT tools for industrial test systems; by calling, an art & culture manager. I founded and led Shiraz Art House, an independent home for artists in Shiraz, and spent years teaching the classical guitar — chasing the clarity of a single well-played phrase or a single quatrain of Khayyám. Different rooms, one mind.

That same patience runs through two separate tracks: my day work as a software engineer, and — apart from the job — my master's research at TU Clausthal, the slow, honest work of getting a battery model to tell the truth. I'm a project manager by training too, and — first and always — a guitarist, teacher and composer. The thread through all of it is one: curiosity, craft, and building things that bring people together. Learn to live, live to learn.

The causes I give my time to are simple: education, children, arts & culture, and animals — the same four threads that run through my work on Sahar (free education for kids who are out of school) and through two cats who run my household.

Research & papers

Teaching machines to read time.

A master's thesis and a small portfolio of papers, all circling one question: how early can we tell what a system will become? Most are battery prognostics; a few reach toward how people learn.

Master's thesis · TU Clausthal
Active M.Sc. thesis · TU Clausthal · in write-up

Early-Cycle Battery Lifetime Prediction

Variable-length transformers that estimate a lithium-ion cell's remaining useful life from only its first cycles — benchmarked against recurrent and convolutional baselines with a reproducible, multi-seed pipeline.

Papers & experiments
Draft

Early Signals, Distant Outcomes

One model that predicts remaining life early — and reaches across domains, from batteries to jet-engine turbofans. A draft, with its first result figure taking shape.

In progress

Head-to-Head with the Baselines

An honest comparison against the established battery models — where a single variable-length transformer wins in the early regime, and where the specialists still lead.

In submission

Foundation Model vs. From-Scratch

An honest study: for battery RUL, a big pretrained model does not beat a small purpose-built one — and costs far more to run. A note heading to a preprint.

Null result

DiffBatt+ · A Baseline, Pushed

Reproduced a strong diffusion baseline and tested one idea to improve it — the improvement didn't hold up. A null result, reported plainly.

Exploring next cross-chemistry transfer, cell counterfactuals, reverse-time pre-training, fusing optical & electrical sensing, and the learning-science of thinking well with AI — early ideas, not results yet.

A living portfolio, honestly staged — the thesis is in write-up and one note is heading to a preprint; each item is labelled by its real stage. Numbers and papers appear only once they are public.

Shiraz Art House

A home for art.

Founder and former CEO of an independent house for art in Shiraz, Iran.

Shiraz Art House (خانهٔ هنر شیراز) was an independent, officially registered non-commercial cultural institute in Shiraz, Iran (reg. no. 2907) — a gallery, a stage for music and theatre, a café and a bookshop under one roof, with room for cinema and visual art. It existed to give independent and emerging artists a place to make and show their work, and to bring people together around culture.

I founded and built the house and led it for roughly five to six years as its managing director and CEO. We ran a sustained programme across music, theatre, cinema and visual art, with many partners and recurring projects. For me it was never just a building; it was an open house for independent artists. It was later sold and operated by others.

Beyond the house itself, I've been a patron of independent cinema and festivals — including the Spring Festival (جشنواره بهارهٔ خانهٔ هنر شیراز), which ran under the house's management, and quiet backing that let at least one independent filmmaker's project get made. Not a filmmaking career of my own — just an open door for other people's films.

Shiraz House · خانهٔ هنر — Archive

A cultural history, kept with care.

A respectful archive of the cultural life around Khaneh Honar — its cinema nights, art and music — as remembered history.

This archive gathers the documented cultural history of the Khaneh Honar art centre in Shiraz: a place where, over many years, people came together for cinema nights, exhibitions and live music. It is offered here as cultural memory — a record of programmes and evenings that brought a community around art.

The aim is simple and gentle: to remember the events themselves — the films screened, the works shown, the music played — and the spirit of an independent space for the arts. Nothing here is a claim about ownership or any disputed matter; it is only a record of cultural activity, kept with care.

Music & voice

From the string to the word.

From classical guitar to the recited word.

I'm a classical guitarist and teacher with over 18 years behind the instrument. My playing earned first place at Iran's national Art Olympiad (music) and second at the National Guitar Festival, and I hold an MA in Art Research. I've taught at Forough Music Academy and served on the Music Policy Council of Fars Province.

Much of what I play is bound to Persian poetry — the quatrains of Omar Khayyám and others — where the guitar and the spoken word meet. It is the same love of sound and meaning that runs through everything I make.
