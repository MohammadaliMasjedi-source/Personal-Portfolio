— About

I refuse to fit in one box.

Mo
— Mo

I'm Mohammadali “Mo” Masjedi — from Shiraz, now in the Harz mountains of Germany.

I work where culture and technology meet, because to me they are two languages for the same questions — about beauty, freedom, and how we live well together. I spent years building an independent home for artists in Shiraz and teaching the classical guitar, chasing the clarity of a single well-played phrase or a single quatrain of Khayyám.

Now I bring that same patience to engineering: the slow, honest work of getting a battery model to tell the truth. The thread through all of it is one — curiosity, craft, and building things that bring people together. Learn to live, live to learn.

Research & papers

Teaching machines to read time.

A master's thesis and a small portfolio of papers, all circling one question: how early can we tell what a system will become? Most are battery prognostics; a few reach toward how people learn.

Master's thesis · TU Clausthal

Early-Cycle Battery Lifetime Prediction

Variable-length transformers that estimate a lithium-ion cell's remaining useful life from only its first cycles — benchmarked against recurrent and convolutional baselines with a reproducible, multi-seed pipeline.

Battery & time-series prognostics

Early Signals, Distant Outcomes

One model that predicts remaining life early — and generalises across domains, from batteries to jet-engine turbofans.

Foundation Model vs. From-Scratch

An honest study: for battery RUL, a big pretrained model does not beat a small purpose-built one — and costs far more to run.

Cross-Chemistry Transfer

Making a battery model still work on chemistries it never saw in training, with only a few examples to adapt.

Counterfactual Cell Post-Mortems

“Why did this cell die?” — the smallest early-cycle change that would have flipped its predicted lifetime.

Reverse-Time Pretraining

Reading a battery's history backwards, as self-supervision, to learn a stronger early-life fingerprint.

Shiraz Art House

A home for art.

Founder and former CEO of an independent house for art in Shiraz, Iran.

Shiraz Art House (خانهٔ هنر شیراز) was an independent cultural space in Shiraz, Iran — a gallery, a stage for music and theatre, a café and a bookshop under one roof, with room for cinema and visual art. It existed to give independent and emerging artists a place to make and show their work, and to bring people together around culture.

I founded and built the house and led it for roughly five to six years as its managing director and CEO — confirmed in Iran's official state gazette as its CEO from 2017 to 2019, and addressed by the provincial culture authority as its director. We ran a sustained programme across music, theatre, cinema and visual art, with many partners and recurring projects. For me it was never just a building; it was an open house for independent artists. It was later sold and operated by others.

Cinema & film

Images as a way of thinking.

In the tradition of Iranian film — patient, humane, close to ordinary life.

09
Music & voice

From the string to the word.

From classical guitar to the recited word.

I'm a classical guitarist and teacher with over 18 years behind the instrument. My playing earned first place at Iran's national Art Olympiad (music) and second at the National Guitar Festival, and I hold an MA in Art Research. I've taught at Forough Music Academy and served on the Music Policy Council of Fars Province.

Much of what I play is bound to Persian poetry — the quatrains of Omar Khayyám and others — where the guitar and the spoken word meet. It is the same love of sound and meaning that runs through everything I make.
