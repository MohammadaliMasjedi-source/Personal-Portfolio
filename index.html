(02) — Research

Battery lifetime, predicted early.

My thesis asks a deceptively simple question: how long will this cell live? — and answers it from only the first handful of charge–discharge cycles, so testing that once took a year can inform a decision in weeks.

01

The problem

Batteries reveal their lifespan slowly. Waiting for a cell to fully degrade is expensive and slow. The goal: forecast remaining useful life from early-cycle signals.

02

The method

A variable-length transformer encoder, benchmarked head-to-head against LSTM, GRU, CNN and a pooled baseline under one fair, multi-seed protocol.

03

The insight

Attention shines when there's context to attend to — the more early-cycle data the model sees, the more its advantage grows, and the attention maps reveal which cycles matter.

04

Across chemistries

Unifying six public sources spanning multiple cell chemistries — studying what transfers between them, and what stubbornly does not.
