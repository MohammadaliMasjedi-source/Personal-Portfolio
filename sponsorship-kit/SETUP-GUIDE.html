a Ko-fi ~10 min — recommended first

Why first: free 0%-platform-fee tier, PayPal-linked, instant, no approval wait.

  1. Go to ko-fi.com and click Sign Up.
  2. Sign up with email (fewer permissions than "Continue with Google/GitHub").
  3. Choose your page handle (see above).
  4. Settings → Payments — connect your PayPal (or Stripe). You enter your own details directly with Ko-fi.
  5. Confirm you're on the free 0%-fee plan (default — the paid "Gold" tier is optional).
  6. Copy your handle into js/support.js → SUPPORT.kofi = "your-handle", commit + push. Live in about a minute.
Fee: 0% Ko-fi fee. Your processor still takes its normal cut (~2–3%).

b Buy Me a Coffee ~10 min

  1. Go to buymeacoffee.com, click Start my page.
  2. Sign up, pick your page handle.
  3. Settings → Payments — connect Stripe or PayPal.
  4. Copy your handle into js/support.js → SUPPORT.buyMeACoffee, commit + push.
Fee: 5% platform fee + processor's normal cut.

c PayPal.me ~5 min — if you already have PayPal

  1. Go to paypal.me and claim your link (uses your existing PayPal login — does not create a new account).
  2. Pick your PayPal.me handle.
  3. Copy it into js/support.js → SUPPORT.paypalMe, commit + push.

No existing PayPal account? Skip this one — opening a brand-new account is a bigger step (bank linking, ID verification), outside this guide's scope.

Fee: standard PayPal receiving fee (varies by country/currency).

d GitHub Sponsors ~20–30 min + review wait

  1. Go to github.com/sponsors, click Set up GitHub Sponsors (also under your profile → Sponsor area, or Settings → Sponsors).
  2. Fill in your sponsors profile: short bio, what support funds (you can honestly mention Sahar here — GitHub's profile explicitly supports "what this funds" text).
  3. Connect payouts via Stripe Connect: bank account + tax residency. Germany is a supported payout country — this is exactly where the tax-classification question shows up (see the legal box below).
  4. Submit. GitHub reviews new sponsored accounts before the button goes live — can take a few days, out of your control once submitted.
  5. Once approved, the button appears wherever FUNDING.yml references github: [your-handle], and on your profile.
Fee: 0% GitHub platform fee for individual sponsors (Stripe's normal processing fee still applies).

⚖ Legal honesty box — read before turning anything on

In Germany, income from sponsorships/donations — even small recurring ones — is taxable, and if it becomes systematic it can be classified as self-employment. That's the same Blue Card Zusatzblatt gate already flagged in the finance plan: a side activity (Nebentätigkeit) needs approval before it starts. One-off small gifts are lower-risk but still technically declarable.

Recommendation: set the accounts up now — free, zero income flowing, zero classification risk — then flip "receiving" on (put your handle into support.js/FUNDING.yml) only once the Zusatzblatt gate clears. Rails ready the day it's legal, not before.

Once past that gate: Kleinunternehmerregelung (§19 UStG) — under the annual revenue threshold, no VAT charge/report needed (simpler bookkeeping); income tax still applies regardless. Not tax advice — confirm your case with a Steuerberater before real money moves.
